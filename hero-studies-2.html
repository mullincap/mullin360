DThe reel

The hero never stops moving.

Five works cross-dissolve on an eleven-second cycle, each riding a slow camera push. The headline stays fixed and absolutely still while the work moves behind it. A ruled index ticks along the foot, naming what you're looking at — so the hero isn't decoration, it's the portfolio, passing.

  • Continuous motion — there is never a frozen frame
  • Cross-dissolves at 2s, pushes at 11s: slow enough to feel like film, not a carousel
  • No single weak photo has to carry the whole first impression
  • The named index ties the hero to The Record — W.01, W.02, W.04…

The most motion, and the most content. This is what Hines and Related actually do.

EThe camera move

One continuous pull-back.

We open pushed hard into Camelback Mountain in a tight letterbox — you cannot even tell it's a dealership — and then the camera pulls back over five seconds while the aperture opens, revealing the whole site. The headline sets itself line by line during the move, rising from behind its own mask. One gesture, not a stack of effects.

  • A genuine camera move: scale 2.05 → 1.02 with a matched aperture opening
  • The mountain — the only monumental thing in the frame — is what you meet first
  • Kinetic type: each line rises from behind a mask, the italic arriving last
  • Depth of field fades in at the end, as the shot settles

The most cinematic single shot. It hides the photo's weakness by not showing it until you're already sold.

FThe slats

The site assembles itself.

The image is built out of seven vertical columns that rise in sequence, each seam flaring seal-green as it lands — the frame going up the way a building goes up, bay by bay. Then it settles and drifts, endlessly and almost imperceptibly, so it never freezes. The most structural of the three, and the most obviously "designed."

  • Seven columns, 120ms apart — architectural rhythm, not a wipe
  • Each seam flares green as its column lands, then fades: the seal, seven times
  • A 40-second drift afterwards, so the hero is never a still
  • Pure CSS — one background image sliced by background-position

The most kinetic. Risk: it can read as an effect rather than a photograph. Watch it twice before you decide.
